Barren Star

By Arturo Pérez Roncero

Chapter 1: The Dust

The regolith of Kepler-186f was a fine, rust-colored powder that got into everything. It coated Kaelen’s visor, seeped into the seals of his suit, and formed a permanent gritty film on the inside of his helmet. For three standard years, he had been the sole inhabitant of this world, a single human mote in an ocean of silent, ochre dust. His job was simple: prospect. Find anything of value—rare metals, unique isotopes, anything that would justify the colossal expense of sending him here. So far, he had found nothing but disappointment.

His scanner chirped, a weak, hesitant sound that was different from the usual monotonous drone. He paused, the twin suns of the system casting his long shadow across the crater floor. The signal was deep, buried beneath meters of rock and dust. It wasn't a metallic signature, nor was it radioactive. It was... something else. Something organic, the scanner claimed, though Kaelen knew that was impossible. This world was sterile, dead, barren. It was in the name they gave it back on Earth.

Chapter 2: The Anomaly

It took him a week to dig. The Hab's automated excavator whined in protest as it clawed through compacted rock. Finally, it struck something that wasn't rock. It was smooth, black, and absorbed the light of his floodlights completely. It was an obelisk, impossibly geometric, its surface devoid of any markings. The organic signature was pulsing from its core, a slow, rhythmic beat like a slumbering heart.

When he touched it, his gloved hand sinking slightly into its surface as if it were liquid, the universe fell away. He saw nebulae born and stars die. He saw worlds teeming with life he couldn't comprehend, and he saw them wither into dust, just like this one. He felt a consciousness, ancient and vast, reaching out, not with words, but with pure concepts: loneliness, hope, and sacrifice.
